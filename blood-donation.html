About Blood Donation

Every day, thousands of people need blood transfusions to survive. Your donation matters.

Why Donate Blood?

Blood is needed every two seconds in the United States alone. It's essential for surgeries, cancer treatment, chronic illnesses, and traumatic injuries. There's no substitute for human blood - it can't be manufactured.

Despite medical advances, the need for blood is constant. Only 3% of age-eligible people donate blood yearly. One donation can save up to three lives.

The donation process is safe, simple, and takes less than an hour. Most healthy adults can donate blood every 56 days.

Donor Eligibility

Before you donate, please check if you meet these basic requirements

Age & Weight

You must be at least 17 years old (16 with parental consent in some states) and weigh at least 110 pounds.

Health Condition

You must be in good health and feeling well on the day of donation. Free from cold, flu, or other infections.

Frequency

You can donate whole blood every 56 days. Platelet donations can be made more frequently.
