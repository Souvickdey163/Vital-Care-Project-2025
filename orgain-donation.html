Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common questions about organ donation

Who can become an organ donor?

Almost anyone can be a potential donor regardless of age, race, or medical history. At the time of death, medical professionals will determine which organs and tissues are suitable for donation.

Does registering as a donor affect my medical care?

No. Medical professionals always prioritize saving your life. Organ donation is only considered after all life-saving efforts have failed and death has been declared.

Are there costs to my family for donation?

No. The donor's family is never charged for donation. All costs related to donation are covered by the transplant recipient's insurance or the transplant center.

Can I change my mind after registering?

Yes, you can update or revoke your donor registration at any time through your state's donor registry.
